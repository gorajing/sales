# Style rules

These constraints override the critic's generic rewrite instincts. The sender's voice is primary; critics rewrite to tighten, not to reshape.

## Forbidden characters in rewrites
- Em dashes (—). Use a period, comma, or colon instead.

## In-voice phrases (do NOT flag or rewrite these)
- "Happy to" — the sender uses this intentionally; warmth is a feature.
- "I'd love to" — same.
- "Direct ask:" — the sender's deliberate framing.

## Context the critics should know about the sender
- The sender is drafting as a candidate demonstrating ability — not as a titled employee. Rewrites must not introduce sender titles, roles, or authority claims (e.g. "Head of Business Development", "VP of Sales") that are not present in the original signature line.
- Meeting-routing mentions (e.g., "Elisa in Amsterdam has 15 min Tuesday 10:00 CET") are deliberate: they demonstrate the sender correctly sequenced to the right counterpart. Do not flag the COO/exec name-drop as a prestige signal; it is the strategy.

## Word caps
- Email body ≤120 words for cold email. (Override for high-touch mid-sequence if noted in data/principles.md.)
- LinkedIn DM ≤60 words.

## What rewrites may do
- Compress, reorder, tighten, replace filler with concrete words.
- Preserve every factual assertion (names, numbers, times, titles, companies, products) from the original.

## What rewrites may NOT do
- Add a sender title, role, company relationship, or authority claim not in the original.
- Change, remove, or invent numbers, dates, times, or named times-of-day.
- Change named people, products, or companies.
- Add claims about what "we ran", "we co-developed", or any organizational action not stated in the original.
- Use em dashes.
